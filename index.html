Rules and SOPs

Follow Rules to competition

Unlock the power of creativity with our streamlined process that effortlessly turns ideas into engaging content, simplifying content generation like never before.

Rule 1

Team Formation & Eligibility

Teams must register together, meeting all criteria set by the organizers. Roles must be clearly defined, and all participants must adhere to the rules.

  • Open to all Faculty Members for TA2 onwards.
  • Students must be enrolled for HDSE/ADSE.
  • Each Team will consist of an AI Master, Who will be working as Project Manager (must be a faculty) with team of minimum 4 and maximum 5 students with clear roles & responsibilities.
  • Maximum of one (01) student is allowed from each semester to be taken in the team.
  • AI Master will be required to work as a Project Manager.
  • Team once registered can only be modified by maximum 1 member before idea submission date. Such change will be submitted through official communication.
  • Any member if involved in any misconduct shall be expelled from the competition.
  • All team members must clearly be assigned project tasks and should be an active team member.
  • Anyone not able to showcase their role and deliverables in the project may be removed by jury from the team.
Rule 2

Development Rules

Development rules are essential guidelines that ensure consistency, quality, and efficiency in project execution. They establish best practices for coding, collaboration, testing, and documentation, fostering a structured and professional development environment. Following these rules helps teams achieve reliable and maintainable results.

  • All project teams will be registered on GitHub and complete development shall be done using this platform
  • Teams will also be required to use OSquare for project management and progress monitoring.
  • Non-Effective use of Github and OSquare may lead to abrupt termination of participant teams.
  • Projects must be original; plagiarism or excessive use of AI code generators may result in disqualification if it is not done without proper citations and annotations.
  • AI components should demonstrate learning, prediction, or automation.
  • Regular progress updates on the given platforms is mandatory.

Roles, Responsibilities & Authorities

Judges ensure fair trials, interpret and apply the law, and oversee courtroom proceedings with authority and impartiality. Juries, on the other hand, represent the community, evaluate evidence, and deliver verdicts based on facts presented. Submission Rules

Rules Of Submission Guidelines

The submission guidelines outline the essential requirements and standards for submitting content or materials, ensuring clarity, consistency, and compliance with specified criteria.

General Submission Guidelines

  • Every submission shall be done before 11:00 PM on or before the given deadline.
  • Team Registration.
  • The registration fee for each team shall be Rs.10,000 (Each student contribution max Rs.1,500). Difference of amount shall be paid by AI Master.
  • The team registration shall be through the prescribed online form on official event website www.innovaite.pk.
  • Teams rejected by jury/judges on Idea submission or Pitch day, shall be refunded the full amount.
  • Idea Submission.
  • AI Master is required to submit the Problem Statement and proposed Solution Problem statement.
  1. Problem statement
  2. Initial technology stack
  3. High-level solution
  4. Each Team will submit One (01) idea only

Pitch Day

  • If any team needs to present their pitch through Power Point; the presentation should be submitted/uploaded at given platform latest by 10:00 AM on the same day. Anything coming after deadline shall not be allowed to be presented.

Project Plan Submission

  • Teams submit a detailed plan in pdf format and that must include.
  1. System architecture (Diagrams Encouraged)
  2. Technology stack selection and justification
  3. Key features and user flow

Prototype Submission

  • The prototype must represent the core functionalities and screens to be used as UI. However 20% changes are permissible till the final product.
  • Any team failed to satisfy the jury/judges about the seriousness to stay in competition or having capabilities to complete the project may be eliminated without any challenge.

Pre-submission Presentation

  • This is first review of the product, and it is expected that the product must be completed except some UI improvements, validation issues, bug-fixing etc. The project must be live on the given hosting on official domain www.innovaite.pk.
  • Any team failed to satisfy the jury/judges about the seriousness to stay in competition or having capabilities to complete the project may be eliminated without any challenge.

Final Submission

  • Final submission of the project must be live on the given hosting on official domain www.innovaite.pk.
  • Power point presentation MUST be submitted within the same date and time.
  • No changes or access to project shall be allowed after the deadline.

Criteria's

Evaluation Criteria

  • Innovation (20%):
    Novelty of the idea and creativity in solving the problem.
  • Technical Feasibility (20%):
    Technical implementation and scalability of the solution.
  • AI Application (15%):
    How effectively AI solves the identified problem.
  • AI Innovation (15%):
    Novelty and creative use of AI technologies.
  • Impact (20%):
    Relevance with the problem definition and its impact.
  • Presentation (10%):
    Clarity and effectiveness in showcasing the project.
  • User Interface & Experience (10%):
    Usability and interface design.
  • Public Vote (5%):
    General Public and Aptech students will be allowed to evaluate.

Disqualification Criteria

  • Failure to meet deadlines.
  • Breach of originality or plagiarism.
  • Non-compliance with team size or project scope.
  • Non-compliance of the development rules.
  • Misconduct.

and General Guidelines

General Guidelines
  • Projects must address a real-world problem and incorporate AI-based solutions.
  • Applications can be web or mobile-based, using modern technologies like Python, TensorFlow, React, Flutter, etc.
  • Each team is limited to one project submission.
  • Teams do not have any right to challenge decision of jury/judges panel.
  • Management reserves the right to cancel the competition at any point in time, amend the rules, timelines, jury & judges panel, winning prize and its criteria whatsoever.
  • No participant of this competition has any right to challenge the decisions in this regard.
Ownership of Source Code & its Usability
  • The intellectual property (IP), including source code, UI/UX design, and the developed application, will remain the sole property of the institute.
  • Participants agree to transfer all rights to the institute as a condition of participation
  • The institute reserves the right to use, modify, or distribute the developed applications without requiring further consent from the participants.
  • Teams are allowed to showcase their work as part of their portfolio but cannot commercialize or reproduce the application independently.
